# Translation Rules — matrixdestiny.co

## Core rule: never auto-translate

No page may be published as a machine translation of another language version.

Each language version is adapted — not translated. The target language version must:
- Use natural phrasing native to that language's register and rhythm
- Adapt emotional framing to cultural norms around how that emotion is discussed
- Adapt relationship terminology to how that culture names relational dynamics
- Adapt psychological language to the vocabulary that culture uses for inner experience

A PT-BR page that reads like translated Portuguese is not acceptable. It must read as written by a fluent native speaker for a Brazilian audience.

## PT-BR tone rules

**Register:** Accessible and warm, with psychological precision. Brazilian Portuguese in this domain uses direct emotional language without clinical coldness. The reader is a real person trying to understand something difficult about their life — write for that person.

**Specific adaptations:**
- Psychological terms: favor "padrão" (pattern) over "comportamento" (behavior) when describing recurring dynamics
- "Bloqueado" for blocked arcana — this is established vocabulary in the PT-BR matrix of destiny community
- Relationship language: PT-BR uses warmer, more relational framing than European Portuguese — adapt accordingly
- Section headers: translate the emotional state, not the arcana concept alone. "Por que você..." is the correct frame for H1s.
- FAQ questions: always use "você" (informal) — never "o senhor/a senhora"

**Never in PT-BR:**
- European Portuguese vocabulary (e.g., "autocarro" instead of "ônibus")
- Machine-translated syntax: "é uma coisa importante a notar" (is an important thing to note)
- Anglicisms where a natural Portuguese term exists

## Ukrainian tone rules

**Register:** Direct and precise. Ukrainian psychological and personal development content uses a more measured tone than Russian-language content in the same domain. Avoid Russian calques — use native Ukrainian vocabulary throughout.

**Specific adaptations:**
- Use "архетип" (arkhetip) for archetype — this is the established term
- "Заблокований" for blocked arcana
- "Матриця долі" — two words, "ь" in "долі" — use consistently
- Emotional vocabulary: Ukrainian has rich terminology for internal states — use it rather than defaulting to Russian-influenced terms
- Section headers: use the emotional state framing equivalent to "Чому ви..." (Why you...)
- Address the reader as "ви" (formal you) — not "ти" (informal) — this is the standard in Ukrainian personal development content

**Never in Ukrainian:**
- Russian vocabulary when Ukrainian equivalents exist
- Surzhyk (Russian-Ukrainian mixed language)
- Content that reads as translated from Russian rather than written in Ukrainian

## English tone rules

**Register:** Precise and psychologically grounded. No hedging, no mystical softening. The English reader for this content is typically someone who has encountered matrix of destiny through social media or an app and is investigating whether it has real explanatory power. Write for that skeptical-curious reader.

**Specific adaptations:**
- Psychological framing: use behavioral descriptions over spiritual descriptions. "Creates patterns of decisional paralysis" over "blocks your sacred life path"
- Active vs. blocked: in English, "active" and "blocked" are the established terms — use them consistently
- Avoid British English spellings — use American English throughout (this aligns with the primary English search market)
- H1 framing: direct question or problem statement. "Why You Always Choose Wrong or Can't Commit" — not "The Lovers Arcana: Meaning and Interpretation"
- FAQ: write as if answering a real person's real question — not as a generic definition exercise

**Never in English:**
- "This powerful archetype" — lazy filler
- "Ancient wisdom teaches us" — mystical padding
- Passive constructions as avoidance: "It is often found that" → "You"
- Hedging that destroys precision: "may sometimes potentially indicate" → just say what it does

## What adaptation means in practice

**Bad (literal translation from PT-BR):**
> "Arcano 8 na Matriz do Destino representa a capacidade de ler causalidade com precisão" translated as:
> "Arcana 8 in the Matrix of Destiny represents the capacity to read causality with precision"

This is actually fine for content — the issue is when structure and framing are also copied verbatim.

**Bad (translated structure):**
PT-BR H1: "Arcano 4 — O Imperador: Por que você não consegue estabelecer limites ou perde o controle"
EN H1 (bad): "Arcana 4 — The Emperor: Why You Can't Establish Limits or Lose Control"

EN H1 (correct): "Arcana 4 — The Emperor: Why You Can't Set Boundaries or Keep Losing Control"

The difference: "establish limits" is a calque. "Set boundaries" is the natural English phrase for this concept. Adaptation means using the phrase native to the target language's register for this emotional domain.

## Language file ownership

Each language version is owned by its language — changes to the English version do not automatically propagate to PT-BR or Ukrainian. Each language version must be independently reviewed for cultural accuracy.

When a structural change is made to the English template (new section, changed FAQ format), the equivalent change must be made in PT-BR and Ukrainian versions — but the content must be adapted, not copied and translated.

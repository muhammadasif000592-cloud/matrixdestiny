# Arcana Semantic Differentiation — matrixdestiny.co

**Purpose:** Prevent ontology drift at scale. Every arcana page must govern exactly one psychological domain. This file defines what each arcana governs AND what it does not govern, with notes on the pairs and clusters most likely to bleed into each other during content generation.

Read this before writing any arcana page. When in doubt about whether content belongs to an arcana, check the NOT-GOVERNS column first.

---

## Critical Differentiation Clusters

These are the groupings most likely to produce content bleed. Each cluster shares surface-level similarity but governs distinct psychological mechanisms.

---

### Cluster A: The Authority / Consequence / Regulation Trio

| Arcana | Governs | Does NOT Govern |
|---|---|---|
| **8 — Strength** | Power dynamics; the dominance-submission axis; capacity to delegate without anxiety; calibrated self-assertion; control-vs-surrender tension | Cause-and-effect in relationships; emotional regulation; nervous-system intensity; impulse management |
| **11 — Justice** | Consequence recognition; proportional accountability; cause-and-effect reading; pattern repetition from unowned contribution; relational reciprocity; victim vs over-responsibility axis | Power dynamics; emotional explosions or suppression; window of tolerance; authority hierarchies |
| **14 — Temperance** | Emotional regulation; nervous-system balance; window of tolerance; explosion vs suppression cycling; intensity metabolism; somatic processing | Power dynamics; authority structures; cause-and-effect accountability; pattern repetition from unowned choices |

**Common bleed errors:**
- Writing 8 as "learning from consequences" — that is 11.
- Writing 11 as "anger management" or "emotional explosion" — that is 14.
- Writing 14 as "power over impulses" framed as authority — that is 8.
- Writing 8 as "emotional regulation under pressure" — Strength governs the *structural* relationship to power, not the nervous-system response to intensity.

| Arcana | Governs | Does NOT Govern |

### Cluster F: The Inner Knowing / Perception Pair

| Arcana | Governs | Does NOT Govern |
|---|---|---|
| **2 — The High Priestess** | Non-rational intelligence; reading situations below conscious threshold; trusting inner perception against external pressure | Anxiety about what others think (18); solitude as processing space (9); unconscious compulsion (15) |
| **18 — The Moon** | Fear projection; anxiety without concrete threat; distorted perception from fear; imagining worst-case in ambiguous situations | Accurate inner knowing that is correct but unvalidated (2); genuine intuition that turns out to be right (2) |

**The key distinction:** High Priestess perceives *accurately* below conscious threshold. Moon perceives *inaccurately* because fear fills the gaps. These are opposite psychological mechanisms that can appear identical from the outside.

## Arcana 18 — Moon Governance Blacklist

Moon governs ONLY:
- how ambiguous information becomes perceptual reality
- fear-generated gap-filling
- the collapse between interpretation and perception
- projection pole / dissociation pole under ambiguity

Moon does NOT govern — these belong to other archetypes:
- emotional flooding → Arcana 14 Temperance
- compulsive attachment → Arcana 15 Devil
- social withdrawal → Arcana 9 Hermit
- consequence accountability → Arcana 11 Justice
- power calibration → Arcana 8 Strength
- will paralysis → Arcana 12 Hanged Man
- structural collapse → Arcana 16 Tower

Any content about hypervigilance, overthinking, anxiety, or uncertainty must stay anchored to the perceptual formation mechanism — not drift into emotional regulation, compulsion, or withdrawal territory.

## Arcana 12 — Hanged Man Governance Blacklist

Arcana 12 governs ONLY:
- developmental suspension
- perspective inversion through enforced pause
- transitional non-movement
- framework invalidation before reorientation
- temporally bounded pause before genuine direction shift

Arcana 12 does NOT govern:
- emotional flooding → Arcana 14 Temperance
- perceptual distortion under ambiguity → Arcana 18 Moon
- compulsive attachment → Arcana 15 Devil
- chosen renewable solitude → Arcana 9 Hermit
- irreversible identity ending → Arcana 13 Death
- structural collapse → Arcana 16 Tower
- generalized fear or anxiety → Arcana 18 Moon
- initiative failure or willpower → Arcana 1 Magician

Anti-romanticization rule:
Hanged Man is NOT "stillness is wisdom."
The active form is correctly identifying whether suspension
is still developmentally productive — and moving when it is not.

### Cluster I: The Cycle / Completion Pair

| Arcana | Governs | Does NOT Govern |
|---|---|---|
| **10 — Wheel of Fortune** | Recognizing which phase of a cycle is active; extracting specific lessons from each phase; maintaining orientation across favorable and difficult periods | Endings and completions (13, 21); suspension during transition (12); the karmic consequence of unlearned patterns (11) |
| **21 — The World** | Genuine cycle completion; integration of what was built; the felt sense of arriving; inhabiting completion before the next cycle | Mid-cycle navigation and pattern recognition (10); the pivot into new beginnings (22); partial-cycle endings (13) |

**The key distinction:** Wheel is mid-cycle navigation; World is cycle completion and integration. A person stuck in a repeating cycle is a Wheel problem. A person who achieves and never feels satisfied is a World problem.

## Content Generation Rules Derived from This Document

1. **One domain per page.** If you find yourself writing content that belongs to two different arcana, you have a domain leak. Stop and identify which arcana owns each element.

2. **Active pole ≠ "good at everything."** The active pole of an arcana is specific to that arcana's domain. Do not write "active Arcana 8 is good at regulating emotions" — that is Arcana 14's active pole, not 8's.

3. **Blocked pole ≠ "bad in general."** The blocked pole describes the specific failure mode of this arcana's domain. Arcana 8 blocked produces dominance-submission cycling. It does not produce "emotional outbursts" — that is Arcana 14 blocked.

4. **Cross-reference numbers encode domain relationships.** When an arcana works-with or conflicts-with another, the content must explain WHY those domains interact — not just list the numbers. If the explanation doesn't make domain-level sense, the cross-reference may be wrong.

5. **FAQ questions must stay within the arcana's domain.** A FAQ question in Arcana 11 asking "how do I manage emotional outbursts" has leaked into Arcana 14. Check every FAQ question against the domain boundary table above.

6. **The karmic pattern must name the original source of the domain's block, not trauma in general.** Arcana 8's karmic origin is binary power distribution. Arcana 14's karmic origin is environments where emotional intensity was dangerous. These are different environments and different adaptations — do not generalize.

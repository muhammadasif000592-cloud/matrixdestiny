# RULE 40 — ARCHETYPE ADAPTATION LAYER

**Purpose:**
Adapt Retrieval Engineering / Rule 40 enforcement for symbolic-psychological ontology systems.

This layer extends the existing Rule 40 framework for:
- Matrix Destiny,
- archetype systems,
- symbolic psychological entities,
- compatibility systems,
- emotional-state retrieval,
- and AI-extractable behavioral interpretation.

The goal is not only ranking.

The goal is:
- semantic extraction,
- AI citation eligibility,
- archetypal differentiation,
- retrieval compression,
- behavioral mechanism clarity,
- and ontology stability across multilingual systems.

---

# RULE 40A — ARCHETYPE GOVERNANCE

Every archetype must govern:
- a distinct psychological mechanism,
- a distinct behavioral pattern,
- a distinct interpretive process,
- or a distinct developmental conflict.

No archetype may overlap heavily with another.

If two archetypes can describe the same behavior using interchangeable language, ontology repair is required.

---

# RULE 40B — MECHANISM-FIRST WRITING

Archetypes must be explained through:
- mechanisms,
- observable behavior,
- interpretive processes,
- decision patterns,
- relational dynamics,
- and developmental logic.

Avoid:
- mystical abstraction,
- generic spirituality,
- personality-label writing,
- vague emotional language,
- and symbolic filler.

Bad:
- "This arcana creates confusion."

Good:
- "This arcana resolves ambiguous information toward feared interpretations before conscious verification occurs."

---

# RULE 40C — ONTOLOGY BOUNDARY ENFORCEMENT

Every archetype page must explicitly differentiate itself from nearby archetypes.

Examples:
- Moon ≠ intuition (High Priestess)
- Strength ≠ accountability (Justice)
- Temperance ≠ suppression (Devil)
- Hanged Man ≠ withdrawal (Hermit)

Boundary drift is prohibited.

If content begins absorbing neighboring archetype domains, rewrite immediately.

---

# RULE 40D — BEHAVIORAL OBSERVABILITY

Every major concept must connect to:
- observable actions,
- recognizable relational patterns,
- professional manifestations,
- emotional reactions,
- communication tendencies,
- or decision structures.

Avoid purely abstract interpretation.

Readers and retrieval systems must both identify:
- how the pattern behaves,
- where it appears,
- and what operational consequences it creates.

---

# RULE 40E — DEVELOPMENTAL CAUSALITY

Karmic or developmental sections must explain:
- why the mechanism formed,
- what adaptive function it originally served,
- why it persists into adulthood,
- and how the original environment shaped interpretation.

Avoid generic trauma language.

Bad:
- "Childhood wounds caused fear."

Good:
- "The nervous system learned to interpret delayed feedback as instability because consequences were inconsistent or unreadable during development."

---

# RULE 40F — EXTRACTIVE ARCHETYPE DEFINITIONS

Every archetype requires:
- a direct definition block,
- extraction-ready summary sentences,
- behavioral summaries,
- and operational distinction language.

Definitions must work independently when extracted by:
- AI overviews,
- LLMs,
- answer engines,
- featured snippets,
- or semantic retrieval systems.

---

# RULE 40G — MACHINE-CONFIDENCE LANGUAGE

Use deterministic operational phrasing whenever psychologically defensible.

Prefer:
- "resolves ambiguity toward feared outcomes"
- "interprets autonomy as threat"
- "substitutes guilt for correction"

Avoid excessive hedging such as:
- "might"
- "sometimes"
- "can occasionally"

unless uncertainty is genuinely required.

Retrieval systems prioritize confidence and specificity.

---

# RULE 40H — CHUNK INDEPENDENCE FOR ARCHETYPES

Each section must remain understandable when extracted independently.

A reader or AI system should not require:
- previous sections,
- hidden symbolic assumptions,
- or contextual reconstruction.

Each chunk should independently communicate:
- the mechanism,
- behavioral consequence,
- and archetypal relevance.

---

# RULE 40I — SEMANTIC DENSITY ENFORCEMENT

Low-information paragraphs are prohibited.

Every paragraph should contribute at least one of:
- behavioral insight,
- mechanism clarification,
- differentiation logic,
- relational manifestation,
- developmental explanation,
- or operational example.

Avoid:
- decorative prose,
- repetitive reframing,
- symbolic fluff,
- or pseudo-depth.

---

# RULE 40J — RETRIEVAL MATRICES

High-value archetypes should include:
- behavioral comparison tables,
- state-recognition matrices,
- pattern differentiation grids,
- and operational scenario comparisons.

Example:

| Situation | Active Moon | Projection Pole | Dissociation Pole |
|---|---|---|---|
| Delayed response | waits for more information | assumes rejection | emotionally disconnects |

Structured retrieval blocks improve:
- AI extraction,
- answer compression,
- passage ranking,
- and semantic confidence.

---

# RULE 40K — QUERY-STATE TARGETING

Archetype pages must target:
- emotional search states,
- relational confusion,
- identity conflict,
- behavioral repetition,
- and developmental uncertainty.

Users search because:
- they are confused by recurring patterns,
- trapped in emotional loops,
- struggling in relationships,
- overwhelmed by ambiguity,
- or trying to understand internal contradictions.

Content must align with those retrieval states.

---

# RULE 40L — LEXICAL TERRITORY GOVERNANCE

Every archetype must maintain:
- a primary semantic territory,
- protected conceptual vocabulary,
- and explicit non-governed domains.

Example:

## Moon governs:
- ambiguity interpretation
- perceptual filling
- projection
- uncertainty distortion

## Moon does NOT govern:
- emotional flooding
- compulsive attachment
- consequence calibration
- authority regulation

This prevents ontology bleed.

---

# RULE 40M — SYNTACTIC DIVERSITY ENFORCEMENT

Ontology consistency must NOT create stylistic uniformity.

Avoid repetitive cadence patterns such as:
- "the mechanism by which..."
- "the process through which..."
- "the specific point where..."

Behavioral precision should remain stable while sentence architecture varies.

Semantic identity must remain consistent without creating detectable AI repetition patterns.

---

# RULE 40N — MULTILINGUAL ONTOLOGY CONSISTENCY

All language versions must preserve:
- archetypal mechanism,
- psychological axis,
- behavioral structure,
- and semantic boundaries.

Translation drift is prohibited.

However:
- cadence,
- cultural tone,
- paragraph rhythm,
- and rhetorical density

may adapt per language.

Ontology consistency matters more than literal phrasing symmetry.

---

# RULE 40O — SYMBOLIC SYSTEM STABILITY

The system must behave as:
- an interconnected semantic graph,
- not isolated content pieces.

Every archetype relationship must reflect:
- mechanism compatibility,
- developmental alignment,
- or structural opposition.

Avoid relationship assignments based only on:
- thematic similarity,
- aesthetic resemblance,
- or symbolic atmosphere.

---

# FINAL ENFORCEMENT — ARCHETYPE RETRIEVAL ENGINEERING

The objective is not generic spiritual content.

The objective is:
- low-cost semantic extraction,
- AI citation eligibility,
- psychologically operational interpretation,
- ontology stability,
- and retrieval-efficient symbolic architecture.

If content:
- creates archetypal overlap,
- weakens mechanism clarity,
- increases interpretive ambiguity,
- introduces semantic bleed,
- or reduces extractive precision,

it must be rewritten immediately.
